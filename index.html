                        The Agro-Industrial Problem
                        
Oil Accumulation in Elaeis guineensis

                    

                

                

                    In Malaysia, the oil palm (Elaeis guineensis) is harvested as Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB). Premature harvesting means transporting bunches filled mostly with water rather than lipid-rich mesocarp tissue, reducing the mill's overall Oil Extraction Rate (OER). 
                

                

                    Mesocarp oil synthesis begins roughly 100 days after anthesis (DAA) and continues rapidly up to 160 DAA. To maximize profitability, we must identify the exact stage when the rate of accumulation is still high versus when it stalls—a classical application of differential calculus in biological growth curves.
                

                

                    
Key Insight

                    

                        By calculating the first derivative $f'(x)$ of oil concentration over time, agronomists can pinpoint exactly when synthesis plateaus and fruitlets begin to detach—marking the biologically perfect harvest moment.
                    

                

            


            
            

                

                    

                        
                    

                    

                        Mathematical Representation
                        
The Logistic Growth Function

                    

                

                

                    The accumulation of mesocarp oil ($y$, in %) as a function of time ($x$, in Days After Anthesis) fits a sigmoidal 3-parameter Logistic Growth model:
                

                
                
                

                    

                        
Biological State Equation $f(x)$:

                        

                            $$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$
                        

                    

                    

                    

                        
First Derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Synthesis):

                        

                            $$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$
                        

                        

                            Or simplified as: $f'(x) = k \cdot f(x) \left(1 - \frac{f(x)}{L}\right)$
                        

                    

                


                
                

                    

                        L = 50%
                        Max Mesocarp Oil
                    

                    

                        k = 0.15
                        Specific Rate Constant
                    

                    

                        x₀ = 130 DAA
                        Midpoint / Inflection Point
